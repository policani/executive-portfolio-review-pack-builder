# Executive Pack Builder

## Build sequence

1. Normalize the inputs.
2. Group records by initiative.
3. Classify each signal as routine status, decision, risk, blocker, dependency, capacity pressure, sponsor action, or weak signal.
4. Summarize the portfolio condition in plain executive language.
5. Build a decision agenda with owners, timing, options, and consequences of no decision.
6. Create initiative cards only for items needing attention.
7. Summarize risk, dependency, capacity, and sponsor-action themes.
8. Summarize value signals when supplied: realized value, maturing value, value at risk, unsupported claims, finance-validation needs, decisions required, and required owner actions.
9. Draft the post-review follow-up register.
10. Add human-decision reminders.

## Executive standard

Lead with what changed, what needs a decision, what is blocked, where commitments are at risk, and what must be followed up.

Do not certify ROI, savings, revenue, or risk reduction. Present finance-sensitive claims as provisional unless explicit human finance validation is supplied, and do not treat delivery completion as value realization.

Executive value narrative should answer:

- What value is evidenced?
- What value is still maturing?
- What value is at risk?
- What claim is unsupported or proxy-only?
- What finance, sponsor, or measure-owner validation is needed?
- What decision is required now?
